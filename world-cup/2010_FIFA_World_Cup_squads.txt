The [[2010 FIFA World Cup]] was an international [[association football|football]] tournament held in South Africa from 11 June until 11 July 2010. The 32 national teams involved in the tournament were required to register a squad of 23 players; only players in these squads were eligible to take part in the tournament.

Before announcing their final squad for the tournament, teams were required to name a preliminary squad of 30 players by 11 May 2010, 30 days before the start of the tournament. With the exception of those involved in the [[2010 UEFA Champions League Final]], the players listed in the preliminary squad were then subjected to a mandatory rest period from 17 to 23 May 2010. The preliminary squad would then have to be cut to a final 23 by 1 June 2010 (midnight [[Central European Time|CET]]).<ref>{{cite news |title=FIFA releases information on squad lists for South Africa 2010 |url=http://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/newsid=1206808/index.html |work=FIFA.com |date=7 May 2010 |accessdate=1 June 2010 |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20100528094003/http://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/newsid=1206808/index.html |archivedate=28 May 2010 <!--DASHBot--> |deadurl=no }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.worldcup2010southafrica.com/world-cup/2010-news/world-cup-2010-prelim-squad-list/ |title=World Cup 2014 Brazil &#124; World Cup 2010 complete preliminary squad list |publisher=Worldcup2010southafrica.com |date=14 May 2010 |accessdate=18 September 2012}}</ref> Replacement of seriously injured players is permitted until 24 hours before the team in question's first World Cup game, though replacement players do not have to be drawn from the preliminary squad.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fifa.com/mm/document/tournament/competition/56/42/69/fifawcsouthafrica2010inhalt_e.pdf |title=Regulations 2010 FIFA World Cup South Africa |work=FIFA.com |publisher=Fédération Internationale de Football Association |location=Zürich |pages=33–35 |format=PDF |accessdate=9 June 2010 |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20100614213414/http://www.fifa.com/mm/document/tournament/competition/56/42/69/fifawcsouthafrica2010inhalt%5fe.pdf |archivedate=14 June 2010 <!--DASHBot--> |deadurl=no |date=February 2010}}</ref>

Players marked ([[Captain (association football)|c]]) were named as captain for their national squad. Number of caps, players' club teams and players' age as of 11 June 2010, the tournament's opening day.

For the first time in World Cup history, all teams had at least one player from a European club ([[Korea DPR national football team|Korea DPR]] being the only team with just one, [[Hong Yong-Jo]]). Three national squads were made up entirely of players from domestic clubs: [[England national football team|England]], [[Italy national football team|Italy]] and [[Germany national football team|Germany]]. [[Nigeria national football team|Nigeria]] was the only team with no players from domestic clubs.

The [[England national football team|English]], [[Germany national football team|German]], and [[Italy national football team|Italian]] squads were made up entirely of players from the respective countries' domestic leagues. The [[Nigeria national football team|Nigerian]] squad was made up entirely of players employed by overseas clubs. 
Although [[Russia national football team|Russia]], [[Turkey national football team|Turkey]], and [[Scotland national football team|Scotland]] failed to qualify for the finals, their domestic leagues were represented by 14, 14, and 10 players respectively. Altogether, there were 52 national leagues that had players in the tournament.
